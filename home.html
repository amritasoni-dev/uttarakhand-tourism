Chardham Yatra
Kedarnath
  • One of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Located at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) in the Garhwal Himalayas.
  • Situated near the origin of the Mandakini River.
  • Surrounded by majestic snow-capped Himalayan peaks.
  • Believed to have been built by the Pandavas and revived by Adi Shankaracharya.
  • Represents faith, spirituality, and the rich heritage of Uttarakhand.
Yamunotri
  • Located at an altitude of approximately 3,293 meters (10,804 feet).
  • Dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, the goddess of the Yamuna River.
  • Famous for its natural hot water springs, especially Surya Kund.
  • Pilgrims cook rice and potatoes in the hot springs as a sacred offering.
  • The temple is accessible through a scenic trek from Janki Chatti.
  • Represents purity, devotion, and the spiritual essence of Uttarakhand.
Badrinath
  • Badrinath Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India.
  • Located at an altitude of approximately 3,133 meters (10,279 feet) in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand.
  • Situated on the banks of the Alaknanda River amidst the majestic Himalayas.
  • Believed to have been established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century.
  • Surrounded by the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges.
  • Nearby attractions include Tapt Kund, Mana Village, and Vasudhara Falls.
  • Symbolizes devotion, spiritual enlightenment, and the rich cultural heritage of Uttarakhand.
Gangotri
  • Gangotri Temple is dedicated to Goddess Ganga, the goddess of the holy River Ganges.
  • Located at an altitude of approximately 3,100 meters (10,200 feet) in Uttarakhand.
  • Situated on the banks of the Bhagirathi River, the primary source stream of the Ganges.
  • According to Hindu mythology, King Bhagirath performed penance here to bring the Ganga to Earth.
  • The temple was built in the 18th century by the Gorkha General Amar Singh Thapa.
  • Represents purity, devotion, and the spiritual significance of the Ganges in Indian culture.
